Gastronomy

Asado
Asado is one of Argentina's most iconic dishes and is at the heart of Mendoza's gastronomy. It is a barbecue of meat, typically cuts of beef, pork, and lamb, grilled with a characteristic smoky flavor. In Mendoza, asado is often accompanied by chimichurri, a sauce made from garlic, parsley, vinegar, and oil.
Empanadas
Empanadas are another typical Argentine dish that can be found in abundance in Mendoza. They are small pastries filled with meat, chicken, ham and cheese, spinach, or corn, among other ingredients. Each region of Argentina has its own variation of empanadas.
Locro
Locro is a thick and flavorful stew made from corn, meat, sausage, and vegetables. It is especially popular during the winter and is served with chopped green onions and hot chili pepper.
Chivito a la llama
Mendoza is famous for its kid (young goat) meat, which is prepared in various ways. "Chivito a la llama" is a special preparation where the kid is cooked over an open flame, giving it a unique flavor
Regional Dishes
In addition to the more well-known dishes, Mendoza offers a variety of regional dishes including "humita en chala" (corn tamales), "pastelitos" (fried sweets filled with quince or sweet potato), and "picadas," which are platters of cheeses and cold cuts accompanied by wine.
Wines
Of course, one cannot talk about Mendoza's gastronomy without mentioning its wines. In addition to Malbec, the region produces a wide range of red and white wines that perfectly complement the local cuisine. You can enjoy wine tastings at the numerous wineries in the region.
